Site and surroundings

Not reviewed

The application site comprises a two-storey Victorian mid-terrace dwelling constructed of yellow London stock brick with a slate roof and original timber sash windows to the front elevation. The rear garden extends approximately 12 metres and is enclosed by timber fencing to the side boundaries. The property is flanked by similar two-storey Victorian terraced dwellings at Nos. 1 and 3 Desendans Road, with No. 3 having an existing single-storey rear extension. To the rear, the site backs onto the gardens of properties fronting Elmwood Avenue.

The site is located within the Green Village Conservation Area. A mature oak tree subject to a Tree Preservation Order (TPO/2019/0042) stands in the rear garden of No. 3, with its canopy extending partially over the application site. The property also falls within the buffer zone of Southwark Woods Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI), located approximately 150 metres to the east.

The surrounding area is predominantly residential in character, comprising Victorian and Edwardian terraced housing. Desendans Road is an unclassified road within a 20mph zone. Local amenities including shops and a primary school are situated on Green Village High Street, approximately 200 metres to the north, and the area benefits from good public transport links with Green Village Station within a 5-minute walk.

Summary of advice

Not reviewed

This is the case officer's summary of the conclusions and advice.

Your proposal for a rear conservatory is likely to be supported in principle, however there are changes you will need to make before submitting a formal application. The conservatory frames must be timber or slim-profile aluminium rather than uPVC to comply with conservation area requirements. The depth of the extension should be reduced from 4 metres to 3 metres, or incorporate a 45-degree splay on the boundary with No. 3, to avoid unacceptable impact on your neighbour's amenity. Most critically, the current footprint encroaches into the root protection area of the protected oak tree at No. 3 and cannot be supported. You will need to commission an arboricultural impact assessment and redesign the footprint to sit entirely outside the RPA, or propose construction methods that avoid root damage.

Requirements

Not reviewed

The following documents and information will be required when you submit your formal planning application.

Drawings

Elevation drawings

The drawings should show the building's outside now and after the proposed changes.

Make sure you:

  • submit an individual elevation drawing for the front, back, and both sides of the building
  • show any proposed buildings (including household extensions and outbuildings) in context with neighbouring buildings
  • scale plans at 1:100 or 1:50 on A4 and A3 paper size
  • include a scale bar on each plan
  • include a unique reference number on each drawing
  • include written dimensions, especially for proposed extensions
  • include details of materials used in the development
Floor plans

Floor plans should show the internal layout of the existing and proposed building.

Make sure you:

  • include existing and proposed plans
  • scale plans at 1:100 or 1:50
  • include room dimensions and uses
  • show the relationship between the extension and existing rooms
Site plan

A site plan showing the proposal in relation to site boundaries and other buildings on site.

Make sure you:

  • scale at 1:200 or 1:500
  • show the direction of north
  • show the proposed extension in relation to site boundaries
  • include the position of any trees on or adjacent to the site

Supporting

Design and access statement

Required for applications within a conservation area.

Make sure you:

  • explain the design principles and concepts
  • demonstrate how the proposal preserves or enhances the conservation area
  • describe the materials and finishes proposed
Arboricultural impact assessment

Required due to the protected oak tree at the neighbouring property.

Make sure you:

  • commission a report from a qualified arboriculturist
  • include a tree constraints plan showing root protection areas
  • demonstrate that the proposal will not harm the protected tree
  • include a method statement for construction if working near the RPA

Evidence

Photographs

Photographs of the existing property and surrounding context.

Make sure you:

  • include photos of the rear elevation where the extension is proposed
  • include photos showing the relationship with neighbouring properties
  • include photos of the protected tree
