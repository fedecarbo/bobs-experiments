Erection of a single storey rear extension and associated internal alterations.

Back

Design and conservation

Height and massing

Needs changes

Policy references: London Plan D9, Southwark Plan P14

The proposed building height of 8 storeys (28m AOD) exceeds the prevailing 4-6 storey context along this section of the high street. While the site is identified as appropriate for increased density, the current massing does not respond sensitively to the established building heights.

To address this, the applicant should consider:

  • Stepping back the upper 2 floors from the primary frontage by a minimum of 3 metres
  • Reducing the overall height to 6 storeys at the corner junction
  • Providing a more gradual transition in scale towards the adjacent 3-storey terrace

Materials and detailing

Complies

Policy references: Southwark Plan P13

The proposed multi-stock brick finish and traditional window proportions are appropriate for the conservation area context. The soldier course detailing and recessed window reveals demonstrate a high quality of design.

Impact on listed building

Does not comply

Policy references: NPPF 199-202, Southwark Plan P19

The proposed development would cause less than substantial harm to the setting of the Grade II listed St Mary's Church (list entry 1234567). The harm arises from the scale and proximity of the new building, which would diminish the prominence of the church spire in views from the north.

In accordance with paragraph 202 of the NPPF, this harm must be weighed against the public benefits of the proposal. At this pre-application stage, it is not clear that the public benefits would outweigh the identified harm.

The applicant should explore design revisions to reduce the impact, including lowering the building height in the zone closest to the listed building and increasing the setback from the boundary.
